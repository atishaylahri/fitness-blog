The fitness tracker market has matured significantly. What cost $200 three years ago is now available for under $80, and the accuracy gap between budget and premium devices has narrowed considerably. If you're not looking for smartwatch features like app notifications and contactless payments, a dedicated fitness tracker in the sub-$100 range is genuinely hard to beat.

Here's our pick of the best fitness trackers under $100 in 2026 — all tested for accuracy, battery life, and everyday usability.

## Fitbit Charge 6

The Fitbit Charge 6 is the best all-round fitness tracker at this price point. Google's acquisition of Fitbit has brought meaningful improvements — the Charge 6 now integrates with Google Maps, Google Wallet, and YouTube Music controls. More importantly, the health tracking is excellent: Active Zone Minutes, 24/7 heart rate, SpO2 monitoring, and ECG app (where available).

**Key specs:** Heart rate, SpO2, ECG, GPS, 7-day battery, Google integration, AMOLED display
**Best for:** Anyone in the Google ecosystem, all-round health trackers
**Typical price:** $79–$99

[Check Price on Amazon](https://amazon.com?tag=gymjar-20)

The Fitbit app remains one of the best in the category — the Sleep Score and Daily Readiness Score features alone make it worth the price.

## Garmin Vivosmart 5

Garmin's budget tracker punches well above its class in health monitoring. The Vivosmart 5 includes Body Battery (Garmin's energy level score), stress tracking, Pulse Ox, respiration rate, and advanced sleep tracking with sleep stages. The display is minimal — a slim OLED strip — but it's readable even in direct sunlight.

**Key specs:** Heart rate, SpO2, Body Battery, stress tracking, 7-day battery, slim profile
**Best for:** Serious fitness data enthusiasts, Garmin ecosystem users
**Typical price:** $79–$99

[Check Price on Amazon](https://amazon.com?tag=gymjar-20)

Garmin's Body Battery score is genuinely one of the most useful metrics in wearable technology — it tells you whether your body is ready to train hard or needs rest, based on HRV, stress, and sleep quality.

## Xiaomi Mi Band 8

For pure value, nothing comes close to the Mi Band 8. Under $40 in most markets, it offers an AMOLED display, 16-day battery life, 150+ workout modes, heart rate and SpO2 monitoring, and surprisingly accurate step counting. It lacks built-in GPS (uses phone GPS), but for most people that's a reasonable trade-off at this price.

**Key specs:** 1.62" AMOLED, heart rate, SpO2, 150+ workouts, 16-day battery
**Best for:** Budget shoppers, first-time tracker users, teens
**Typical price:** $35–$50

[Check Price on Amazon](https://amazon.com?tag=gymjar-20)

The companion app has improved significantly and now provides clear, actionable data without overwhelming you with numbers.

## Samsung Galaxy Fit 3

Samsung's slim fitness band finally hits a compelling price point in 2026. The Galaxy Fit 3 has a larger display than previous generations (1.6" AMOLED), 13-day battery life, and deep integration with Samsung Health — one of the most comprehensive free fitness apps available. Sleep tracking is particularly strong, with detailed REM/light/deep breakdowns.

**Key specs:** 1.6" AMOLED, 100+ workouts, heart rate, 13-day battery, Samsung Health
**Best for:** Samsung phone users, sleep tracking focus
**Typical price:** $49–$69

[Check Price on Amazon](https://amazon.com?tag=gymjar-20)

If you're in the Samsung ecosystem, the Galaxy Fit 3 integrates beautifully with Galaxy phones and the Health Monitor app.

## Whoop 4.0 (Subscription Model)

Whoop deserves a mention even though the device itself is free — you pay a monthly subscription ($30/month or less on annual plans). The reason it makes this list is that serious athletes often find the insights worth the cost. Whoop tracks HRV, recovery score, strain score, and sleep performance with impressive accuracy. There's no display, no step counting — it's purely for performance optimization.

**Key specs:** HRV, recovery score, strain score, sleep coach, no display, 4-day battery
**Best for:** Serious athletes focused on performance data
**Typical price:** Device free with $199/year subscription

[Check Price on Amazon](https://amazon.com?tag=gymjar-20)

Whoop isn't for everyone — but for athletes who want to know exactly when to push and when to recover, it's the most sophisticated tool in this roundup.

## Final Verdict

For most people, the **Fitbit Charge 6** is the best all-round fitness tracker under $100. The combination of accurate health tracking, Google integration, and an excellent app make it hard to beat. Data-obsessed athletes should look at the **Garmin Vivosmart 5** for the Body Battery feature alone.

On a tight budget? The **Xiaomi Mi Band 8** is extraordinary value — you'd struggle to find a better tracker for under $50. Samsung Galaxy phone users will love the **Galaxy Fit 3** for its deep Health integration.

Whatever you choose, wearing a fitness tracker consistently — even a basic one — leads to meaningful improvements in activity, sleep, and awareness of your own body. The best tracker is the one you'll actually wear every day.
